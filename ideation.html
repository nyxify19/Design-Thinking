The Synthesis: Top 10 Solutions

Filtered from 540 ideas across 5 "How Might We" clusters.

1. Anonymous Digital Support Platform

A confidential app where students share struggles without identity exposure. Directly attacks the stigma barrier — the single biggest reason students don't seek help. Anonymity is the unlock.

2. Peer Support Buddy System

Cross-batch pairing where seniors check in on juniors weekly. Tackles social isolation and converts the toxic peer-competition dynamic into a support dynamic. Zero infrastructure cost.

3. AI-Driven Burnout Alerts

Passive monitoring via daily mood check-in data flags at-risk students automatically. Removes the burden of self-initiated help-seeking — which stigma blocks.

4. "More Than NEET" Alumni Campaign

Alumni who took non-MBBS paths share their stories across media and schools. Attacks the deepest root cause — society valuing only one definition of success.

5. Parent Workshops on Mental Health

The pressure source is the family. No amount of student-side intervention works if parents keep demanding ranks. Educating parents shifts the input, not just the output.

6. Wellness Breaks in Timetable

Structurally protected non-academic time. Students cannot opt into rest if the schedule doesn't permit rest. Institutional, not individual, fix.

7. Faculty Empathy Training

Faculty are the daily touchpoint. A teacher who recognizes burnout signs intervenes early; one who doesn't, accelerates the spiral. Highest-leverage staff intervention.

8. Mandating Counseling Services

Without a mandate, individual colleges treat counselling as optional. Policy converts good practice into a floor-level requirement across all institutions.

9. Balanced Grading (Effort + Marks)

Restructures the incentive system itself. As long as only marks are rewarded, every other intervention fights the grading system. Changing what gets measured changes what students optimize for.

10. Student Wellness Dashboards

Aggregated, anonymized wellbeing data made visible to administration. Creates institutional accountability — what gets measured gets managed. Closes the feedback loop.
