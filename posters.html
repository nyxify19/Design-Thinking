Acknowledgments & Work

Website — Advait

This website serves as the central repository for our design thinking project. It brings together research findings, empathy insights, ideation outputs, and proposed solutions into a single accessible platform — documenting our end-to-end process from problem discovery to prototyping.

Solution Poster — Aditi

A visually structured poster presenting the top solutions our team converged on — including peer support networks, structured mental health check-ins, and faculty sensitisation programs. Each solution is mapped to the specific pain points uncovered during the empathy phase.

Problem Poster, Empathy & Journey Maps, Root Cause Analysis — Satwika

Covers the problem poster articulating the core challenge — extreme academic pressure, isolation, and lack of institutional support. Empathy and journey maps track the emotional arc of students across exam preparation cycles, while the root cause analysis traces systemic contributors to mental health decline in NEET and MBBS contexts.

Awareness Posters — Aniruddh & Kulsum

A series of awareness posters designed to surface the mental health crisis among medical students to a broader audience — peers, faculty, and institutions. The posters translate data and lived experiences into compelling visual narratives that prompt acknowledgment and action.

Prototype — Tanya

An interactive prototype demonstrating how our primary solution would function in a real setting. It simulates key touchpoints such as anonymous help-seeking, peer matching, or faculty reporting pathways — focusing on usability and the student experience.

Top Solutions — Keerthana

A curated presentation of the highest-impact solutions shortlisted through structured ideation and feasibility assessment. Each solution is evaluated against desirability, viability, and implementability — offering institutions a prioritised roadmap for improving student mental health support.
